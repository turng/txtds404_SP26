DENNY'S WAS CLOSED, A SAGA IN PORTLAND
by Verna D.

This is a true and actual (if not always factual) account of my experiences during the weekend of the 7 SECONDS shows,one of which I co-promoted. Some of this information is taken directly from my journal entries at that time. Other parts are embellishedjournal pieces, or taken from memory and filled in as I go now.
I decided to leave out some stuff,because it is too personal for this 'zine, or inappropriate and/or incriminating. Mostly, it is just a personal account of my thoughts at that time, which began Oct 18th, 1986, and ended Oct. 19th (or 20th,actually.). So here goes nothing:

SATURDAY, OCT. 18th, PART ONE — CARRIDE TO OLYMPIA,FROM PORTLAND.

Coffee. The initial buzz is good.My eyes are out and shining . Lucid. No towel,no shower — no dice. I feel like a scummy heap o' NO USE.NO use for me today. Hangin' around like a noose around someone's neck. I hate relying on people.I would rather rely on myself.By the same token I would rather rely on acquaintences than friends,and much better rely on total strangers. I can't get pissed at friends or acquaintences if they disappoint,because I feel bad or guilty or stupid. But strangers can fuck off if they cross me in some way,and I won't care.
This is odd man out time. Talking, acting like I know what I'm doing when in actuality, I feel completely lost inside. Acting tuff,like I'm a big shit,but I totally have no clue.None at all.Big talk but I'm scared. Feeling pretty self-righteous. This is an honest(defensive) feeling but dangerous...
Bad sleep lately.
I want to stare at the sun until I learn to see. Something like that. If I stare at the sun on Hank's back,will it have the same effect?(I been readin' Rollins' Hallucinations of Grandeur, on the way down here Friday, and it stuck in my haid)...
I'm bummin'. I don't know why. Not really as "up" for the show as I would like to be. At this point , I will settle for a few recognizable faces.

(Stopped at a Chehalis,WA gas station/askin' directons to a skate park/I joke about the small hicktown whiteness of the place/"take a left at the crossburnin'"/ this has me goin' even after we leave the place...)

I'm so witty.Pass the chloroform.O.K. Carrying only one tape with me — Flipper BLown' Chunks . Pass the ludes.No seriously.I'm swelling up, gettin' too big for my britches.Cracks,cracks and more cracks. Mouth full of shit.Head full of shit. Riding in this car.With this girl and this guy. How do I know I can trust 'em. How do I know they won't drop me off in Chehalis? And book. Or bail.Book, that's a word used when I was in jr. high school, meaning leave a place. There are so many words for leaving . So many ends to a mean (a way).One mean can have a thousand ends.More than I can think about. I wouldn't write this if I weren't on the road...(on heavy drugs, haha)... when I close my eyes and let the music in it cuts deep. It hurts and feels good all at once...

There are thousands of highways
And thousands of visions
There's more than a million veins
needing to be fed
Hungry for blood(traffic)
Dusk snuggles in and waits
For the dance of darkness to begin
The word dusk reminds me of the word dust
And dust remind s me of the underside of my shell
Which badly needs shaking out

(Flipper intones on the car's tape player) "WE ARE BREEDING"

Darkness coming on as tape advances to my favorite Flipper songs. "In Your Arms",followed by "Life is Cheap."
Night has left me hollow.

PART TWO — SHOW IN OLYMPIA

(Finally here. all right . Startin' to warm up a bit and get a little excited. So I go to the door ,or where they take your money,and I go, "I think I'm on the guest lis". I'm not,but they let me in anyway,because I tell them I'm promoting the Portland show. I guess that was o.k. to do...I don't know. I missed most of Danger Mouse visiting with the Yobs. I barely recognize anybody, and make lots of stupid cracks ,generally being a jerk. Out of sheer nervousness I think.Yeah. What I saw of Danger Mouse I really ,really liked. For one thing, Kurt is an awesome performer, and him and the whole band were fun to watch. I was pretty distracted, which is understandable because of lack of sleep ...Back to the journal.)

Melvins real hot tonight.Smokin' long songs sendin' shivers in me.Woa. By the stage writing. No headbangin'. Neck sore from last nightss Saccharing Trust show. Feelin' just a little bit sick. Don't know why. Just a little.

EARLY MORNING OCT. 19th.

So full of shit last night I thought I would scream. I thought someone would get wise and beat the crap out of me.

Unseen Force were good. Yobs were happening.7 Seconds were hot. Sets were too short. A guy dove off the stage and busted his head maybe. He's like , "Fuck you! I don't want to go to the hospital!" (His choice was either go with the medics or with the cop. Now which would you choose?)
Did Stan ( promoter of the show in Olympia) do okay? I wonder.

8:40 a.m. Sunday.Sleep okay.Not good yet.When will it be? I wonder. I told the bands their soundchecks were at 2 and 3,etc. Fuck is the sound even gonna be there?That would be kind of stupid if it weren't.
This place, this place is a castle. Jeannie and Steven's place is rather the cool spot. Nice. Plushy almost.Roomy enough to hold all three bands. No,not really. Want to get to talk more with Kevin, but why?
The best stuff is what I leave out of the journal. Crap. Invited Jennifer and Kris to the show. Kris won't make it and Jennifer will forget. I feel crummy for lying to her. I lied so much last nightf. I'm just a liar. Panik was there. Good.

I look in the mirror and I hate what I see. I hate what I see ,and can almost like what I hate. I look sick,stupid,and lame.I feel strong ,absorbant,reliable, like a human tampon or paper towel. Kinda bummin'. Fuck! Why!?I feel stupid here, "turning even less than stupid..." Ahahahahahahaha.

PART THREE — THE SHOW. BEFORE AND AFTER.

( This whole thing is written from memory,because I had no time or desire to write in my journal at this time.If it seems fragmented, it is justifiably so, for this is being penned months after the fact.All right?)

The confidence of last night did not carry over to today,the stupidity did. Shouldn't I be doing cocaine or something.? I do not feel like a gonzo promoter at all, and this is a very scary,definitely destructive feeling. Steven is busy doing things, and I'm just sort of sitting here in this huge place, cleaning up a leftover mess because I don't know what else to do.
The Rebel Skates crew is already here,set up for the skate competition. I was worried it was gonna rain,but it's actually a pretty nice day out. There seems to b e a lot of kids for the competition, I wonder if they will all come for the show.
Off to find some food. All they had that I could afford was these greasy fries, hardly a gastronomical delight. Oh well, I need my energy, and it looks like there won't be any coke (or even Pepsi.) So, anyway, this place is pretty cool, think I'lkl l hide out in the band room for a while.
Shit! They announced the contest,telling the skaters they can get in for free with the $4 entry fee to the contest! Fuck, it's s'posed to be the other way around,I thought I made that clear, but oh well.Well, this is just dandy. Where is the money from the show going to come from? Fuck it all to hell.I really don't feel in control now,but Steven (co-promoter) seems to have everything pretty much taken care of.
Well, it kinda sucks that I can't go in the Monqui office ,and that they (Monqui) won't listen to me and will listen to Steven,but they don't really know me, and he works for 'em. So, I paid them (it still sucks). The guy that was supposed to clean never showed up so I just did what I could. It wasn't that gruesome. Things seem to be going wrong left and right. Everyone's late pretty much. At least the sound is here. Maybe they aren't late. The Yobs showed up early and 7 Seconds were pretty much on time, just my Seattle friends were late. Oh well,it's okay.
7 Seconds sounded great in the soundcheck. That woman doin' the sound is great. The sound here is awesome. Loud enough, vocals clear, just great! It's gonna be a good show, if everyone shows up .
Well , all the bands are here,but where are the Fallen Angels. They were supposed to be here an hour ago. Maybe they got a late start.Shit, the doors are supposed to open now. I feel like a complete jerk. My security didn't even show up. What the fuck,the show must go on without them. It's good that some Monqui people,or Steve's friends,are here (thanks Bruno, thanks Bill and others!) or I would be dead.I'm not even worried about the skate thing anymore,too busy worrying about the Fallen Angels not being here. I'm a wreck. Safe in this ticket booth I guess. There seems to be a lot of kids waitin' to get in. That's cool! I'm sellin' lots of tickets which is good, and it seems like most of the skaters are comin' in too. Great! Ah relief,the Fallen Angels got here finally. I am not mad at them, just exasperated in a way...

Well, I"m missing all the bands sittin' out here in this booth. Bruce is here to keep me company and that is great. He is the only stabilising force around me right now , and I can't say how much of a great comfort that is to me. He even bought me an ice tea! What a pal!

Looks like at this preliminary count that I'm about three hundred or more in the hole. The prospect is grim. The show's almost over and it doesn't look like there's going to be many more people showin' up. 7 Seconds is going to be on soon.Some folks comin' in are mad that it's still five dollars. It's not my fault they are late. They don't know I'm in the hole and need every cent I can get to pay everyone off...

I ran out into the room twice during 7 Seconds' set. Once for "Just One Day!" and once for "Somebody Help Me Scream". That was really fun,k intense, awesome, just lettin' loose and dancing and shouting along to those songs. It meant so much to me right then and there, moreso than at any other time that whole day. It was magic,just beautiful.

By the end of the eveningg I felt really, well, insecure. I felt that I had done all the wrong things, that the whole problem centered on what I personally fucked up. I felt bad that I couldn't pay the Yobs what I had promised them, but they were pretty good natured about it. It was fun to hang out with the bands and just listen and feel almost like we were friends, or something silly like that. There are many things I wanted to ask and say, but held back because, well, I don't really know these people, except through their music/ Sometimes I wish I could just express, without fear, what I am thinking, what I am feeling, at the exact moment I am thinking it. Perhaps I would have been better off a drunken groupie, I don't know.

PART FOUR — DENNY'S WAS CLOSED...

Dennyss was closed ! What?! Yes , that's right, three carloads of people(four carlodas?) Like, eighteen fuckin' possible customers and the damn place is closed! I am feeling so strange ,so , just like I'm in a dream or a bad new wave movie.We all want to go congregate somewhere , so , oh, let's just go on the highway and head south. I'm hoping we don't end up in Salem.

We all come to rest at Bob's Big Boy,in Wilsonville,OR.Not a big town. The poor waitress. All of a sudden ,here's thes e people, not g quite what they expected I'll bet. These scraggley, hardcore,not-so-hardcore, not-so-scraggly, pretty boisterous, group of folks. Oh, okay. She handled it pretty well and the scarier people were served by the manager! These kids were mighty hungry. Obligatory Yobs/7Seconds food fight,stolen silverware(okay,so I was feeling desperate!). Lucky for the Bob's Big Boy crew we didn't stay very long.

Obligatory Yobs sendoff/goodbye after the Bob's destruction and thievery. Hugs and kissies and off we all go into the wild night... (And my prose falls apart). WE get in our respective vehicles and mosey on down the highway, Yobs on their way home, and the rest of back to Steve and Jeanniess for rest and chatter.then , sleep.
(end of memory-writing,back to journal)

10/19/86
I lost my shirt,and my pants and my shoes and socks. Itcs hard to let it all sink in.I don't like people much right now.But then I'm sure they like me even less. It's here that my writing falls apart.I'm hittin' the burnin' stuff(vodka) and feel like I don't know who I am really.I never have.Don't know if I ever will.Should I leave.(over at Jeannie and Steven's house/feeling uncomfortable)I kinda want to. The reading tomorrow should be real good,leaving me emptier than ever. I sort of understand,but I don't know. Who do I have to talkk to?No one really knows what's going on inside my head.No one wants to. I know I don't. Lucidity is a lie. I'm susceptible.Hurtin' Haha. O.k., now wait a minute there.Hold on a second.Feeling divorced.Alienated.

10:58 A.M. 10/20/86

Early morning smog walk to Quality Pies was good. The smog is rich.I like it because it allows me to hide.I especially dug it last night. So (around 5 a.m.) I'm in the Q.P. and order a baked potato.I attempt to anyway, but my voice is gone, so the order is a faint whisper, right. So, I'm writing theis letter to Kevin,which became dotted with butter,thinking suicidal as I write,no,but he doesn't need to hear my ramblings at a late nite/all nite diner,foggy from lack of sleep... Augghhh! Had to bail from this house. Nothing to lose.

COUCH PARK

I'm ugly today.Good.Will keep the assholes away.(?) When will I come to an understanding. When will they come to an understanding of me?Never.
Beautiful day out.Children yelling their guts out at Couch Park.The fall leaves remind me of what is right with the world . The smell of their decay fills the air. That smell specific to Autumn. A smell I'm very fond of.
