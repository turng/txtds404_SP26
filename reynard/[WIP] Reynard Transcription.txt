Thus were we quyte of Reynart a longe whyle. Atte laste cam he in lynkes of an heremyte, &amp; brought to me a lettre for to rede, sealed wythe the kynges seal, in whiche stode wreton that the kynge had made pees oueral in his royame, &amp; that alle maner bestis and fowlles shold doo none harme ner scathe to ony other. Yet sayd he to me more that he was a cloysterer or closyd recluse be-comen, and that he wolde receyue grete penance for his synnes. He shewd me his slauyne &amp; pylche, and an heren sherte ther vnder, &amp; thenne sayd he <g=spiky>Syr Chaunteclere, after thys tyme be no more aferd of me, ne take no hede, for I now will ete no more flessh; I am forthon so olde that I wolde fayn remembre my sowle. I wil now go forth, for I haue yete to saye my sexte, none, and myn euensonge; to God I by-take yow <g=round>Tho wente Reynart thens, sayeng his Credo, and leyde hym vnder an hawthorn. Thenne was I glad and mery, and also toke none hede, and wente to my chyldren and clucked hem to gyder, and wente wythout the wal for to walke, wherof is moche harme comen to vs; for Reynart laye vnder a busshe and cam krepyng bitwene vs and the yate, so that he caght one of my chyldren and leyd hym in his male, <pb n="10"/>wherof whe haue had grete harme, for syth he hath tasted of hym ther myght neuer hunter ne hounde saue ne kepe hym from vs. He hath wayted by nyghte &amp; daye in such wyse that he hath stolen so many of my chyldren that of xv I haue but foure, in suche wyse hath this theef forslogen them. And yet yes-terday was Coppen my doughter, that hier lyeth vp-on the byer, with the houndes rescowed. this complayne I to yow, gracious kynge; haue pyte on myn grete &amp; vnresonable damage and losse of my fayre chyldren.

Then spacke the kynge <g=round>Syre Dasse, here ye thys wel of the re-cluse your eme; he hath fasted &amp; prayde that if I liue a yere he shal abye it. Now herke Chaunteelere, youre playnte is ynoughe; youre doughter that lyeth here dede, we wyll gryue to her the dethes right; we may kepe her no lenger, we wil betake her to God. We wylle syngen here vygylie and brynge her wor-shypfully on erthe, and thenne we wille speke wyth thyse lordes, &amp; take counsyl how we may do ryght and justyse of thys grete murdre and brynge thys fals theef to the lawe <g=spiky>Tho begonne thy Placebo Domino, wyth the verses that to longen, whiche yf I shold say were me to longe. Whan this vigilye was don &amp; the commendacion, she was leyde in the pytte, and there vpon was leyde a marble stone polyshed as clere as ony glas, and theron was hewen in grete lettres in this wyse <g=smooth>Coppe, Chanteklers dough-ter, whome Reynart the Foxe hath byten, lyeth hier vnder buryed: complayne ye her, for she is shame-fully comen to her dethe! <g=spiky>After thys the kynge sente for hys lordes and wisest of his counseyl, for to take aduyse how this grete murdre and respaas shold be punysshyd on Reynart the Foxe. Ther was concluded and apoynted for the beste, that Raynart shold be sent fore, &amp; that he lefte not for ony cause, but he cam in to the kinges court for to here watshold be sayd to hym, &amp; that Bruyn the Bere shold doo the message. The kynge thought that alle this was good, and saide to Brune the Bere <g=smooth>Syr Brune, I wyl that ye doo this message but see wel to for your self, for Reynart is a shrewe, and felle, and knoweth so many wyles that he shallye and flatre, and shal thynke how he may begyle, deceyue, &amp; brynge yow to some mock-erye <g=spiky>Tho sayde Brune: What, good lord, late it allone. Deceyueth me the Foxe? so haue I ylle lerned my casus. I trowe he shall come to late to mocque me <g=spiky>Thus departed Brune meryly fro thens, but it is to drede that he cam not so meryly agayn.
